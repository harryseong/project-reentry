State Appellate Defender Office (SADO)

The State Appellate Defender Office represents indigent individuals convicted of felonies throughout the State of Michigan. SADO practices holistic and client-centered lawyering to obtain positive outcomes for clients on appeal. SADO public defenders regularly argue cases in the Michigan Supreme Court and have helped exonerate 19 wrongfully convicted individuals in recent years.

SADO’s Juvenile Lifer Unit represents nearly 200 individuals who are entitled to resentencing after a series of US Supreme Court decisions declaring their sentences unconstitutional. In 2016, SADO formed Project Reentry, an organization aimed at assisting returning citizens transition home from long prison stays.

Project Reentry

SADO’s Project Reentry launched in 2016 in response to the unique and immediate needs of Michigan’s juvenile lifers—individuals who were sentenced as children (age 17 and under) to mandatory life without parole sentences. Today, we provide reentry assistance to all individuals coming home from incarceration.

Project Reentry is staffed by one full-time Reentry Coordinator, Allison Gorine, agorine@sado.org, and is managed by SADO's Deputy Director, Marilena David-Martin, mdavid@sado.org.

Project Reentry’s primary goal is to help achieve positive outcomes for individuals using several tools toward goal achievement:

  • The development of Comprehensive Reentry Plans (available to clients only)
  • The facilitation of monthly Reentry Workshops and bi-weekly Well-Being meetings (available to all)
  • The publication of a monthly Reentry Newsletter (available to all)
  • The publication of a Reentry Guidebook (available to all)
  • The development of this application (available to all)
  • Hands-on post-incarceration assistance (available to all as resources allow)

To learn more about Project Reentry, visit: www.sado.org/go/projectreentry.
